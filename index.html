ABOUT

About Me

My name is *redacted* and I'm an aspiring web developer. Currently I am wrapping up a degree in Computer Science.

Going against the grain, C++ was my very first programming language. At first it was very difficult to wrap my head around. Infact, I remember my first roadblock vividly - the dreaded for loop. After many nights of study and energy drinks, it all started to make sense. As soon as everything began to "click", I fell in love with programming.

So if I'm familiar with C++, why am I working on web development instead? Well, during one of my college semesters, I took a class that focused on devloping websites. Since then, it's reignited a hidden flame inside of me that is an interest in web development. You see, during my childhood, I would occasionally fool around with WYSIWYG editors(Coffee Cup especially). I would use the drag and drop tools to put together very basic websites, but every once in a while I'd type out my own HTML tags. And that made me feel like I was the coolest kid ever.

I hope that's still the case today.

Another reason why I switched to web development is because I'm a visual person. I enjoy putting things together that I can see. I like things that look nice. And that's pretty much it. While I'm not a simple man by any means, sometimes I have simple reasons.

That's all I have to say about me. If I seem cool to you or you like Windows XP as much as I do, feel free to reach out. I look forward to hearing from you.

MY WORK

My Work

Here are a few things I've put together.

Simple Definition Lookup App

My first API usage, a web app built with Javascript and the FreeDicitionary API. I wrote this because it addresses three issues I have with online dictionary websites.

  1. Frequently, I'm looking for just the definition and nothing more.
  2. I don't like being shuffled around between pages.
  3. I want to be able to see what I previously searched without having to browse my history or hit the back button.

This Portfolio Website!

Every portfolio website I've seen online looks exactly the same. A simple , "Hey, I'm *firstName* *lastName* - I'm a *job description* who enjoys *activities*. Let's get in touch, I want to help!"

I'm weird and different. And I wanted my portfolio to encompass that. So instead of having a cookie cutter portfolio website, I decided to replicate Windows XP. Why? I wanted to see how much I could do with Javascript alone. Turns out, quite a bit. For this, I used absolutely no tutorial. Everything from the login screen to the pop up notification to the resizing of the windows, even the immitation login lag - it all just "clicked". I knew EXACTLY what to do, even though I never did any of it before. And that's what kept me going.

Redacted Match Reporter

Built with HTML, CSS, Javascript and some PHP. The purpose of this app was to give players of *Redacted* an easier way to report bad matches. This form will record user submitted information on a text file, which is then forwarded to the game developers periodically. The page refreshes every minute or so, and updates the submissions in real time.

TaniaProductions.com

A website built with HTML, CSS, and plain ol' Javascript. Very proud of the mobile hamburger menu. I figured that one out without a tutorial.

Mason's Helping Paw

A website made for a local nonprofit cat rescue operation. My first real experience with responsive design for multiple devices.

Rock Paper Scissors

Wanting to practice my DOM skill and JavaScript, I put together a simple Rock Paper Scissors game. It counts how many times you play, how many times you've won, and calculates your win percentage.

Calculator

A simple calculator made with HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Some features are missing, and there are bugs, but when are there never? An absolute disaster, and my very first DOM based project. Actually, it's my first ever Javascript project. Definitely in need of a do-over.
