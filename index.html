ABOUT

About Me

My name is Sanobelk and I'm an aspiring web developer. Currently I am finishing my degree in Computer Science and Information Security.

Unlike most people who get into programming, C++ was my very first language. At first, like anything else, it was difficult. Very difficult. Infact, I remember my first roadblock vividly - the dreaded for loop. After many nights of repeated study and energy drinks, it began to make sense. As soon as everything "clicked", I fell in love with programming and realized that I can overcome anything. Before learning to program, I tended to give up very easily. If I was met with resistance, I threw my hands in the air.Programming has taught me that I can do anything I set my mind to. And Web Development is one of them.

So if I started with C++, why am I working on web development? During one of my college semesters, I took a class that focused on devloping websites. Since then, it's reignited a hidden flame. You see, during my childhood, I would fool around with WYSIWYG editors (Coffee Cup especially). I would use the drag and drop tools to put together very basic websites, but every once in a while I'd type out my own HTML tags. And that made me feel like I was the coolest kid ever. Now that I'm older, and information is readily accessible online, I chose to give it another shot.

Another reason why I switched to web development is because I'm a visual person. I enjoy seeing the fruits of my labor. I also like things that look nice. And that's pretty much it. While I'm not a simple man by any means, sometimes I have simple reasons.

Why Aren't You Using Your Real Name or Photograph?

A simple answer which has four reasons.

  1. I grew up being told not to reveal personaly identifiable information - and for good reason. Now, more than ever, Big Tech collects and sells your data. Data is such a hot commodity right now. Many will pay for it. I also enjoy my anonymity. I don't like what happens in the real world to follow me online, and vice versa. Just like how you're supposed to keep work and play separate, I keep online and offline separate as well.
  2. If for some reason, someone misconstrues an action or statement of mine, thanks to the Twitter Mob, my life would be destroyed in an instant. There have been countless lives ruined by Twitter and other Social Media platforms. I refuse to fall into that category.
  3. My identity has been stolen and used for nefarious purposes on an international scale, from China, to Russia and even Venezuela. Many websites I've signed up for in the past have been part of data breaches, and all my personal information has been found online in a number of places, which I've confirmed myself.
  4. I take security very seriously and intend on making that at some point my primary focus in web development when I become more skilled.

There is absolutely nothing wrong with having a minimal online presence. I firmly stand by that statement and will never budge on it.

If I seem cool or you like Windows XP as much as I do, feel free to reach out to me. I look forward to hearing from you.

MY WORK

My Work

Here are a few things I've put together.

Simple Definition Lookup App

My first API usage, a web app built with Javascript and the FreeDicitionary API. I wrote this because it addresses three issues I have with online dictionary websites.

  1. Frequently, I'm looking for just the definition and nothing more.
  2. I don't like being shuffled around between pages.
  3. I want to be able to see what I previously searched without having to browse my history or hit the back button.

This Portfolio Website!

Every portfolio website I've seen online looks exactly the same. A simple , "Hey, I'm *firstName* *lastName* - I'm a *job description* who enjoys *activities*. Let's get in touch, I want to help!"

I'm weird and different. And I wanted my portfolio to encompass that. So instead of having a cookie cutter portfolio website, I decided to replicate Windows XP. Why? I wanted to see how much I could do with Javascript alone. Turns out, quite a bit. For this, I used absolutely no tutorial. Everything from the login screen to the pop up notification to the resizing of the windows, even the immitation login lag - it all just "clicked". I knew EXACTLY what to do, even though I never did any of it before. And that's what kept me going.

Redacted Match Reporter

Built with HTML, CSS, Javascript and some PHP. The purpose of this app was to give players of *Redacted* an easier way to report bad matches. This form will record user submitted information on a text file, which is then forwarded to the game developers periodically. The page refreshes every minute or so, and updates the submissions in real time.

TaniaProductions.com

A website built with HTML, CSS, and plain ol' Javascript. Very proud of the mobile hamburger menu. I figured that one out without a tutorial.

Mason's Helping Paw

A website made for a local nonprofit cat rescue operation. My first real experience with responsive design for multiple devices.

Rock Paper Scissors

Wanting to practice my DOM skill and JavaScript, I put together a simple Rock Paper Scissors game. It counts how many times you play, how many times you've won, and calculates your win percentage.

Calculator

A simple calculator made with HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Some features are missing, and there are bugs, but when are there never? An absolute disaster, and my very first DOM based project. Actually, it's my first ever Javascript project. Definitely in need of a do-over.
